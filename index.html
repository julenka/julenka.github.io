Julia Schwarz

I build software that leverages probabilistic modeling, signal processing, and machine learning to improve user interfaces.

I received a BS from University of Washington, and a PhD from Carnegie Mellon University. In 2012 I co-founded Qeexo, a startup developing "rich-touch" interactive technologies. At Qeexo I led the team that developed the FingerSense technology currently shipping on Huawei devices. Now I work on the HoloLens team at Microsoft.

You can find me on GitHub, Twitter, StackOverflow, and LinkedIn.
